For all the talk of models and fraud, what actually matters to a respondent is four screens. Here is the whole thing they see: the click, the scan, the pass, and the gate opening into your study.

<Steps>
  <Step title="They click your study link" icon="arrow-pointer" iconType="light">
    The check opens from wherever you recruit, a panel invite or an Alchemer or Qualtrics link, before the respondent reaches the study. The screen is plain: "Prove you're real. A quick face check before you continue."
  </Step>
  <Step title="They do the scan" icon="camera" iconType="light">
    A few seconds in the camera with live prompts, a small "hold still" as it reads a real, present face. No photo is shared with you or with your survey platform, only a yes or no.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pass or fail, instantly" icon="circle-check" iconType="light">
    A real, unique person goes straight into your study. Anyone who cannot clear the check is screened out before the survey, so there is nothing to pay for and nothing to clean up later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="When they come back" icon="rotate" iconType="light">
    Return to the same study and they are recognized as someone who already answered, so one person means one response, permanently. Come to a different study and they are recognized with far less friction, cleared for the new one in moments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Barely any friction for the people you want

The whole point is that a genuine respondent hardly notices it. Real people clear the scan in seconds and are recognized on the way back, while the accounts you were about to pay for nothing quietly fall away at the door. It is a filter on who collects, not a hurdle for the people you want in your data.
